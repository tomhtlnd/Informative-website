Global energy generation

The energy transition is a critical challenge facing our planet, as we seek to reduce our reliance on fossil fuels and transition to more sustainable energy sources. However, this transition comes with significant challenges, including the high cost of renewable energy infrastructure and the difficulty of transitioning entire industries to new energy sources. As seen in the graph displayed here, the vast majority of the world's energy generation still comes from fossil fuels.

The worlds capacity

In 2020, the world's energy capacity was dominated by fossil fuels, which accounted for over 80% of global energy production. However, this is slowly changing, as renewable energy sources such as hydro, wind, and solar continue to gain ground. As seen in the bar graph displayed here, hydro power was the second-largest energy source in 2020, followed closely by wind and solar energy.
